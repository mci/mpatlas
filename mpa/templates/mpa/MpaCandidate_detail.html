{% if mpa.name == 'Sargasso Sea - MCBI' %}

The Sargasso Sea is a sea within a sea, a large, slowly turning gyre in the middle of the North Atlantic Ocean. Covering an area of approximately 2 million square miles (5.2 million square kilometers), it is a sea without a coastline. The Sargasso Sea occupies mostly international waters with the exception of a small slice of Bermuda’s Exclusive Economic Zone. Rich in sailor’s lore and home to the Bermuda Triangle, the Sargasso Sea is the nursery of the North Atlantic. Offering food, habitat and refuge, the Sargasso Sea is dotted with large floating mats of Sargassum seaweed. It is the only place on Earth where such large mats form, and these provide a critical habitat for young animals, making it the nursery the North Atlantic. Many of these species are now endangered in their home waters, including four species of sea turtles as well as American and European eels. Bluefin tuna, young sharks and seabirds also rely on this unique floating world in the middle of the Atlantic.
